About the Piano

The piano is an extrodinary instrument created by Bortolomeo Cristofori. The man from Italy was deeply unsatisfied with the lack of control that musicians had over the volume of the, then widely played harpsichord. The main functional difference between the harpsichord and the piano is that, while a harpsichord uses a plucking mechanism to produce sound from the strings, a piano uses a hammer that makes contact with the strings to produce the sound, thus making the piano a percussion instrument!

What is Piano to Me?

My journey with playing the piano started almost 9 years ago when I had just started middle school and wanted to be in band. Our middle school didn't offer keyboard or piano in the band so i played the tuba. But while i played the tuba in school, I wanted nothing more than a piano to play at home. My parents, after much convincing bought me a 200 dollar piano that I would play for hours a day. It was only after 3 years of playing on that piano that I upgrading to the piano that I have today, A Yamaha P71

I was never taught how to play the piano by teachers or parents. My father, in order to put me to sleep as a child would play classical music on the radio next to my crib which he tells me, was the only thing that could put me to sleep. I believe that this habit would eventually flourish into me writing my own pieces on piano years later.
