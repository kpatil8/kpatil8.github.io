About the Piano

Prompt used: "Classical Grand Piano" with deepai.org

The piano is an extrodinary instrument created by Bortolomeo Cristofori. The man from Italy was deeply unsatisfied with the lack of control that musicians had over the volume of the, then widely played harpsichord. The main functional difference between the harpsichord and the piano is that, while a harpsichord uses a plucking mechanism to produce sound from the strings, a piano uses a hammer that makes contact with the strings to produce the sound, thus making the piano a percussion instrument!

What is Piano to Me?

Prompt used: "Digital Piano Black" with deepai.org

My journey with playing the piano started almost 9 years ago when I had just started middle school and wanted to be in band. Our middle school didn't offer keyboard or piano in the band so I played the tuba. But while I played the tuba in school, I wanted nothing more than a piano to play at home. My parents, after much convincing bought me a 200 dollar piano that I would play for hours a day. It was only after 3 years of playing on that piano that I upgrading to the piano that I have today, A Yamaha P71

I was never taught how to play the piano by teachers or parents. My father, in order to put me to sleep as a child would play classical music on the radio next to my crib which he tells me, was the only thing that could put me to sleep. I believe that this habit would eventually flourish into me writing my own pieces on piano years later.

History of Piano Music

Prompt used: "Beethoven Playing the Piano" with canva.com

  • In 1709 Bartolomeo Cristofori invents the piano in Italy.
  • Nearing the ending of the 1700s, composes Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart and Ludwig van Beethoven contribute signification and timeless contributions to the piano and the way it is played.
  • Antonio Vivali Composes "The Four Seasons" between 1718 and 1725
  • At the turn of the 1700's, into the 1800's, the Romantic Era of piano begins and composers such as Frederic Chopin and Franz Liszt revolutize piano playing
  • In 1876, Pyotr Ilyich Thaikovsky's "The Seasons" is completed.
  • Come 1903, One of the most timeless pieces and beutiful pieces, Claire De Lune, is written by Claude Debussy
  • In 1957, A personal favorite of mine, Piano Concerto No. 2 in F major, is written by Dimitri Shostakovich
  • Dave Brubecks "Take Five" popularizes jazz piano in 1959
  • In 1965, Phillip Glass's minimalist composition, "Two Pages" is written, ushering in the modern era of piano
  • Ludovico Einaudi's "Elements" album reachers the top of the classical charts, showing the progress of the modern piano era.

This timeline goes to show that, no matter how long the piano has been invented, it is still being transformed to this day and will continue to be for years to come.
